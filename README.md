## 2. USER STORIES

### User Story 1: Prospective Member Discovery

As a prospective member,
I want to understand what BFF is, what events they run, and what they’ve done in the past,
so that I can decide if joining the club is something I’m interested in.

**Acceptance Criteria**:

- [ ] Homepage displays BFF's mission statement (clear, one-paragraph intro)
- [ ] Main navigation includes links to: Home, About, Events, Members, Apply, etc
- [ ] Clear "Learn More" call-to-action button is visible above the fold
- [ ] Homepage is accessible (readable text contrast, keyboard navigation works)

---

### User Story 2: Member Event Discovery

As an executive member of BFF,
I want to update event information, highlight upcoming meetings, and manage homepage content,
so that the website always reflects current club activity and engagement.

**Acceptance Criteria**:

- [ ] Each event displays: Event Name, Date, Time, Location, "RSVP" or "Learn More" button
- [ ] Events are sorted by date (soonest first)
- [ ] Clicking on an event name/button provides more details
- [ ] Event section is visually distinct from other homepage sections

### Retrospective Questions & Answers

#### Q1: What did you learn from doing our first sprint?

**Answer**:  
We learned that breaking a large project into smaller boxed tasks forces you to prioritize and stay organized. Not everything can be done at once, and some tasks have dependencies, meaning they cannot be completed until other work is completed first. This meant we had to identify what was most valuable to this spring and focus our efforts there, rather than trying to tackle everything simultaneously.

---

#### Q2: What went well? Why?

**Answer**:  
Each team member found their tole within the group during this spring. Everyone completed their assigned tasks smoothly, and when gaps came up, people stepped in to help without needing to be asked. This made our workflow more efficient and showed that our initial role assignments are working well in practice.

---

#### Q3: What didn't go well? Why not?

**Answer**:  
Scheduling meetings was definitely a challenge; with five different schedules, finding times that worked for everyone proved difficult. We also got a slightly delayed start because the IS424 midterm fell right before the spring begam, so the team took a short break before jumping right in. Additionally, working on the codebase remotely created some friction; without in-person communication, team members had slightly different versions and ideas for what the foundation should look like for this milestone. However, once we met in person and talked through our approaches, we were able to allign quickly and move forward.

---

#### Q4: How can you make things better next time?

**Answer**:  
Moving forward, we plan to establish consistent weekly meeting times so scheduling doesnt become a recurring bottleneck. Now that we've aligned on a shared vision for the project, remote work should go much more smoothly since everyone is building from the same baseline. We also want to make better use of the trello board so that all team members can track task progress, see what others are working on, and avoid dupplicating tasks or crossing lines.

---

#### Q5: Are you satisfied with what you already completed?

**Answer**:  
Yes, we are satisfied with what we accomplished. This sprint has taught us a lot about the project and how we work together as a team. We now have a solid deliverable and a strong foundation to build on for the next spring, both in terms of our codebase and our team dynamics.
